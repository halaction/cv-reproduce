\section{Conclusion}

This draft establishes the main reproduction result: in the current CIFAR-10 pipeline, ConvMixer is the strongest of the three evaluated models, ResNet18 is a solid second baseline, and DeiT-Tiny is clearly weaker under the present setup. The current logs also indicate that ConvMixer is slower per epoch than both comparison models, which is an important practical trade-off. The remaining ablation experiments are designed to determine which specific ConvMixer design choices --- patch embedding, channel mixing, and data efficiency --- are responsible for this behavior.
